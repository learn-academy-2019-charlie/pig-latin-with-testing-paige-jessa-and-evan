The Intricacies of Pig Language:

*Hover over each pig to learn from the native speakers*

How do ya' speak pig again?

Let me regale you about the ancient history of the complex dialect of our forefathers.

  • When a human word starts with a vowel, you take that word and add "way" to the end of it.
  • When a human word starts with a consonant, you take all of the consonants in front of the first vowel, move those to the end of the word, and add "ay"
  • Now bare with me, this is when it gets complicated. If the human word contains a 'q', you must treat the subsequent 'u' as if it were a consonant.
  • If the human word contains a 'y' anywhere besides the first letter, then you will treat the 'y' as if it were a vowel.
  • If the human word starts with a 'Y' then you will treat this 'Y' as if it were a consonant.
